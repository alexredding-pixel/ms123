Getting Started
Maritime Sentinel — Cargo Tracking Guide
satellite_alt
1. Connect to live AIS
Click the connection badge in the top-right and paste your free API key from aisstream.io. This enables real-time vessel position updates. No credit card required.
add_circle
2. Add your shipments
Click New Shipment in the sidebar or the + button. Enter your order/container reference, the vessel name and MMSI (look up at VesselFinder.com), and your loading and discharge ports with expected dates.
timeline
3. Track port stops automatically
Each time the vessel declares a new destination via AIS, it appears in your shipment timeline. Port stops are logged 24/7 by the server — even when you're offline.
schedule
4. Understand the ETA column
The ETA shown is always for your final discharge port:
AIS  Direct from vessel navigation — most accurate
+Xd SLIP  Booked date adjusted for accumulated delays
NEXT PORT  AIS ETA is to an intermediate stop, not your port
info
AIS coverage gaps
Terrestrial AIS only covers ~200km from coastlines. Vessels mid-ocean won't update for days. The dashboard uses dead reckoning to estimate their current position — shown as a dimmed marker with an EST badge.
MMSI is the vessel's unique 9-digit identifier. Find it by searching the vessel name on VesselFinder.com or MarineTraffic.com and copying the number from the vessel details page.
